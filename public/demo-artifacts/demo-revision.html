Revision Summary

This revision packet identifies 5 clauses requiring negotiation before execution. The most critical is the 1× monthly liability cap — we recommend a minimum 12× monthly with carve-outs. Secondary priorities are the unilateral modification rights, absence of an SLA, and the 90-day auto-renewal window. All suggested language below is for professional review only and should not be used without attorney sign-off.

Priority Changes

  1. Increase liability cap to 12× monthly fees minimum with data breach and gross negligence carve-outs
  2. Add mutual written amendment requirement for pricing or material feature changes
  3. Add SLA addendum with 99.9% uptime and credit schedule
  4. Reduce auto-renewal notice window from 90 days to 30 days
  5. Add data portability, retention schedule, and certified deletion on termination

Clause Revisions

Critical

Section 9.2 — Limitation of Liability

Issue: 1× monthly cap (~$200) is far below realistic business exposure for data loss or extended outage.

Original Language
"Provider's aggregate liability shall not exceed the fees paid by Customer in the one (1) month immediately preceding the claim."
Suggested (For Professional Review)
"Provider's aggregate liability shall not exceed the greater of (a) the total fees paid by Customer in the twelve (12) months preceding the claim, or (b) [negotiated floor]. This limitation shall not apply to: (i) indemnification obligations; (ii) willful misconduct or gross negligence; (iii) confidentiality breaches; or (iv) data breach involving Customer data."

Why it matters: A $200 effective cap means you have no meaningful recourse for serious failures including data loss, extended downtime, or security breach.

High

Section 12.1 — Modifications

Issue: Allows CloudBase to materially change pricing, features, or terms mid-term with only 30 days' notice.

Original Language
"CloudBase reserves the right to modify the Service or these Terms upon thirty (30) days' written notice. Continued use after the notice period constitutes acceptance."
Suggested (For Professional Review)
"Any modification to the fees or material terms of this Agreement shall require the mutual written consent of both parties. If Customer reasonably objects to any material modification, Customer may terminate this Agreement with a pro-rated refund of prepaid fees for the unused term."
High

Section 8 — Service Levels (to be added)

Issue: No uptime commitment, no SLA, no credit for downtime. All outage risk falls entirely on the Customer.

Suggested SLA Addendum (For Professional Review)
"CloudBase shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure the Service is available 99.9% of the time in any given calendar month, excluding scheduled maintenance. In the event the Service fails to meet the 99.9% uptime commitment, Customer shall be entitled to a service credit equal to 10% of monthly fees for each hour of excess downtime, not to exceed 30% of monthly fees in any single month."
Medium

Section 3.2 — Renewal Terms

Issue: 90-day notice window is unusually long — industry standard is 30–60 days.

Original Language
"...unless either party provides ninety (90) days' prior written notice of non-renewal."
Suggested (For Professional Review)
"...unless either party provides thirty (30) days' prior written notice of non-renewal before the end of the then-current term."
Medium

Section 10.1 — Customer Indemnification

Issue: Overly broad — Customer indemnifies for any claim arising from use, even where CloudBase's own negligence contributed.

Original Language
"Customer shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless CloudBase from any claims, damages, or expenses arising from Customer's use of the Service or Customer's data."
Suggested (For Professional Review)
"Customer shall indemnify CloudBase from third-party claims arising from: (a) Customer's willful misconduct or gross negligence; or (b) Customer data that infringes a third party's IP rights. This obligation shall not apply to the extent the claim arises from CloudBase's own negligence, willful misconduct, or breach of this Agreement."

Negotiation Notes

  • Start with the liability cap — this is the highest-leverage clause. Even getting it to 3× monthly fees is a meaningful improvement.
  • The SLA addendum is often offered as a paid add-on. Ask for it included; if CloudBase insists on charging, evaluate the cost vs. risk.
  • The 90-day renewal window is a negotiating chip — CloudBase may reduce it easily as it has low cost to them.
  • If CloudBase won't budge on unilateral modification rights, negotiate a 'material change' definition and a termination-with-refund right.
  • A GDPR/DPA addendum is typically non-negotiable for EU data — request it upfront if any EU personal data will be processed.
