## Executive Summary

This SaaS Subscription Agreement grants Acme Corp. a non-exclusive, non-transferable license to use CloudBase Inc.'s platform for a 12-month initial term with automatic annual renewal. The agreement presents High overall risk driven by a low liability cap (1× monthly fees), broadly drafted unilateral modification rights, and a 60-day termination-for-convenience notice window that works against the customer. Key financial terms are front-loaded with all fees non-refundable after payment. Legal counsel review is recommended before execution.

## Risk Matrix

### 🔴 CRITICAL — Minimal Liability Cap

**Explanation:** Provider's aggregate liability is capped at 1× monthly subscription fees regardless of the nature or extent of the loss. For an annual $2,400 contract this means the cap is $200 — far below any realistic business damage.

**Why it matters:** If CloudBase loses your data or causes a material business interruption, your maximum recovery is $200 even if your actual loss is far larger.

**Supporting quote:**
> "Provider's aggregate liability shall not exceed the fees paid by Customer in the one (1) month immediately preceding the claim."

**Location:** Section 9.2 — Limitation of Liability  
**Next step:** Negotiate a higher cap — typically 12× monthly fees minimum — and add carve-outs for willful misconduct, gross negligence, and data breach.

### 🟠 HIGH — Unilateral Modification Rights

**Explanation:** CloudBase may modify platform features, pricing, or terms with only 30 days' notice. Customer's only recourse is to terminate — and forfeits any prepaid fees.

**Supporting quote:**
> "CloudBase reserves the right to modify the Service or these Terms upon thirty (30) days' written notice. Continued use after the notice period constitutes acceptance."

**Location:** Section 12.1 — Modifications  
**Next step:** Request that modifications to pricing or material terms require mutual written agreement, not unilateral notice.

---

### 🟠 HIGH — No Service Level Guarantee

**Explanation:** The agreement contains no uptime commitments, no SLA credits, and no remedies for service unavailability. CloudBase's liability for downtime is zero by contract.

**Supporting quote:**
> "THE SERVICE IS PROVIDED 'AS IS' WITHOUT WARRANTY OF ANY KIND, EXPRESS OR IMPLIED."

**Location:** Section 8.1 — Disclaimer of Warranties  
**Next step:** Request a minimum 99.9% uptime SLA with credit schedule.
