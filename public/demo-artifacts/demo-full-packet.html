Part I

Contract Review

Executive Summary

This SaaS Subscription Agreement grants Acme Corp. a non-exclusive, non-transferable license to use CloudBase Inc.'s platform for a 12-month initial term with automatic annual renewal. The agreement presents High overall risk driven by a low liability cap (1× monthly fees), broadly drafted unilateral modification rights, and a 90-day auto-renewal notice window that works against the customer. Legal counsel review is recommended before execution.

Financial Red Flags

High  Non-refundable Annual Prepayment

High  Uncapped Renewal Price Increases

CloudBase can raise prices on renewal with 60 days' notice and no cap. A significant price increase forces Customer to accept or lose prepaid term.

Medium  High Late Fee Rate (18% APR)

1.5% per month is significantly above typical commercial late fee rates of 6–10% APR.

Part III

Executive Memo

MEMO DATE: 2024-01-15

Summary

This SaaS Subscription Agreement presents High overall risk (72/100). DO NOT SIGN without renegotiating the liability cap. The two most serious issues are a $200 effective liability ceiling and unilateral modification rights. Calendar the auto-renewal cancellation deadline immediately: October 15, 2024.

Questions for Lawyer

  • Can we negotiate the liability cap to at least 12× monthly fees, with data breach carve-outs?
  • Will CloudBase accept a mutual written amendment requirement for any fee or feature changes?
  • Is the class action waiver in the arbitration clause enforceable in our jurisdiction?
  • Can we add a GDPR/data processing addendum before go-live?
  • What are our data portability rights at termination?
Part IV

Revision Packet

Priority Changes

  1. Increase liability cap to 12× monthly fees minimum with data breach and gross negligence carve-outs
  2. Add mutual written amendment requirement for pricing or material feature changes
  3. Add SLA addendum with 99.9% uptime and credit schedule
  4. Reduce auto-renewal notice window from 90 days to 30 days
  5. Add data portability, retention schedule, and certified deletion on termination

Clause Revisions

Critical  Section 9.2 — Limitation of Liability

Original
"Provider's aggregate liability shall not exceed the fees paid by Customer in the one (1) month immediately preceding the claim."
Suggested (For Professional Review)
"Provider's aggregate liability shall not exceed the greater of (a) fees paid in the twelve (12) months preceding the claim. This limitation shall not apply to willful misconduct, gross negligence, confidentiality breaches, or data breach involving Customer data."

High  Section 12.1 — Modifications

Original
"CloudBase reserves the right to modify the Service or these Terms upon thirty (30) days' written notice."
Suggested (For Professional Review)
"Any modification to the fees or material terms of this Agreement shall require the mutual written consent of both parties. If Customer reasonably objects, Customer may terminate with a pro-rated refund of prepaid fees for the unused term."

Medium  Section 3.2 — Renewal Terms

Original
"...unless either party provides ninety (90) days' prior written notice of non-renewal."
Suggested (For Professional Review)
"...unless either party provides thirty (30) days' prior written notice of non-renewal before the end of the then-current term."
