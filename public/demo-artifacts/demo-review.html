Executive Summary

This SaaS Subscription Agreement grants Acme Corp. a non-exclusive, non-transferable license to use CloudBase Inc.'s platform for a 12-month initial term with automatic annual renewal. The agreement presents High overall risk driven by a low liability cap (1× monthly fees), broadly drafted unilateral modification rights, and a 60-day termination-for-convenience notice window that works against the customer. Key financial terms are front-loaded with all fees non-refundable after payment. Legal counsel review is recommended before execution.

Risk Matrix

Critical  Minimal Liability Cap

Provider's aggregate liability is capped at 1× monthly subscription fees regardless of the nature or extent of the loss. For an annual $2,400 contract this means the cap is $200 — far below any realistic business damage.

"Provider's aggregate liability shall not exceed the fees paid by Customer in the one (1) month immediately preceding the claim."

Location: Section 9.2 — Limitation of Liability

Next step: Negotiate a higher cap — typically 12× monthly fees minimum — and add carve-outs for willful misconduct, gross negligence, and data breach.

High  Unilateral Modification Rights

CloudBase may modify platform features, pricing, or terms with only 30 days' notice. Customer's only recourse is to terminate — and forfeits any prepaid fees.

"CloudBase reserves the right to modify the Service or these Terms upon thirty (30) days' written notice. Continued use after the notice period constitutes acceptance."

Location: Section 12.1 — Modifications

Next step: Request that modifications to pricing or material terms require mutual written agreement, not unilateral notice.

High  No Service Level Guarantee

The agreement contains no uptime commitments, no SLA credits, and no remedies for service unavailability. CloudBase's liability for downtime is zero by contract.

"THE SERVICE IS PROVIDED 'AS IS' WITHOUT WARRANTY OF ANY KIND, EXPRESS OR IMPLIED."

Location: Section 8.1 — Disclaimer of Warranties

Next step: Request a minimum 99.9% uptime SLA with credit schedule (e.g., 10% credit per 1 hour of excess downtime).

Medium  Auto-Renewal with 90-Day Notice Window

The contract automatically renews annually. To cancel, Customer must give 90 days' prior written notice — meaning the cancellation deadline is October 15, 2024.

"This Agreement shall automatically renew for successive one (1) year terms unless either party provides ninety (90) days' prior written notice of non-renewal."

Location: Section 3.2 — Renewal Terms

Next step: Calendar the cancellation deadline immediately. Consider negotiating a shorter window (30–45 days is typical).

Medium  Broad Customer Data Indemnification

Customer must indemnify CloudBase against any third-party claims arising from Customer's data or use of the platform. This is broadly worded and could expose Customer to significant liability.

"Customer shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless CloudBase from any claims, damages, or expenses arising from Customer's use of the Service or Customer's data."

Location: Section 10.1 — Customer Indemnification

Next step: Narrow the indemnification to claims arising from Customer's willful misconduct or gross negligence.
